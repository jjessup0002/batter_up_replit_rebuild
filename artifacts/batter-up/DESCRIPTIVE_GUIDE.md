# Batter Up — Descriptive Visual Guide

A complete sensory description of every screen, every button, every color, and every interaction in Batter Up. Written so that someone who has never seen the app can close their eyes and still picture exactly what is on the screen.

---

## Part 1: The World of Batter Up

### What you hold
A phone. The app fills the entire screen edge to edge. There is no browser chrome, no status bar clutter — just a clean, full-screen app.

### The feel
Everything is designed for a dugout. The person using it might be wearing batting gloves, might be standing in dirt, might have sunlight glaring off the screen. So every button is large, every label is bold, and the color contrast is aggressive. Nothing is subtle.

### Sound
Every button press gives a gentle, short vibration — a "thump" in your palm. There is no other sound in the app. No chimes, no bleachers crowd noise, no voiceovers.

## Part 2: Colors

### Light Mode (default)

**Background:** A very light gray, almost white but softer. Like a fresh sheet of copy paper, but with a hint of warmth. The hex code is #F5F5F5.

**Foreground / Text:** A dark charcoal, almost black but not harsh. Like a soft pencil line. Hex #212121.

**Primary (the blue):** A strong, saturated royal blue. Not navy, not sky blue — the blue of a clear midday sky. It is the color of action, of confidence. Hex #0A74DA.

**Secondary (the blue-tinted gray):** A pale, icy blue-gray. It is the color of subtle highlights, of cards sitting on the background. Hex #E8F1FB.

**Accent (the gold):** A warm, rich golden yellow. Like the sun hitting a brass doorknob. It draws the eye instantly. Used for scores, for the "Now Batting" banner, for urgent labels. Hex #F9A825.

**Card background:** Pure white. Cards are clean rectangles with rounded corners, floating on the light gray background. Hex #FFFFFF.

**Muted / Borders:** A light warm gray for inactive text and divider lines. Like pencil guidelines. Hex #757575.

**Destructive:** A vivid, aggressive red. The red of a stop sign. Used for errors, strikeouts, and dangerous actions. Hex #D32F2F.

**Success:** A deep, healthy green. Used for positive counts like balls. Hex #2E7D32.

**Navy:** A very dark midnight blue. Used as a background for the game scorecard header and the "Game in Progress" card. In light mode it is #1A2C5B. In dark mode it becomes a brighter electric blue #4DA3FF.

### Dark Mode

**Background:** A very dark charcoal, almost black with a faint blue undertone. Like looking into the night sky over a ballfield after the floodlights go off. Hex #0F1923.

**Cards:** A slightly lighter charcoal. Enough contrast to separate from the background, but still very dark. Hex #1A2535.

**Primary blue:** In dark mode the blue brightens to an electric, almost neon blue. Like a scoreboard LED. Hex #4DA3FF.

**Text:** Soft off-white. Not pure white — that would be too harsh at night. Hex #F5F5F5.

All other colors (accent gold, red, green) stay roughly the same. The gold especially pops against the dark background.

## Part 3: Typography

There are seven text sizes in the app, each with a distinct personality:

- **H1** — 28 pixels, heavy bold. Used for big welcome headings like "Welcome to Batter Up" and "Game Summary." This text commands attention.
- **H2** — 22 pixels, bold. Section titles, card headings, modal titles. "Start Game," "Choose Lineup," "Edit Lineup."
- **H3** — 18 pixels, semi-bold. Sub-headings inside cards, like "Team Stats" or "Top Performers."
- **Body** — 16 pixels, regular weight. The workhorse text. Descriptions, explanations, player names in lists.
- **Label** — 14 pixels, semi-bold. Form labels, button text, small section headers.
- **Caption** — 13 pixels, regular. Metadata, hints, jersey numbers, dates, counts. Smallest text in the app but still legible.
- **Button** — 16 pixels, semi-bold. The text inside every button.

In dark mode the text is slightly softer — off-white instead of near-black — to reduce eye strain.

## Part 4: Icons

The app uses a single icon family called Feather. Every icon is a thin, clean line drawing — no filled shapes, no gradients, no shadows. Think of them as precise pen sketches. Here is every icon you will see:

- **play** — a right-pointing triangle, like a classic video play button
- **play-circle** — the same triangle but inside a thin circle outline
- **rotate-cw** — a circular arrow pointing clockwise, like a refresh symbol
- **calendar** — a small square grid representing a month view
- **bookmark** — a vertical flag shape, like a page marker
- **bar-chart-2** — three vertical bars of different heights, like a bar graph
- **help-circle** — a lowercase "i" inside a circle
- **settings** — a gear with eight teeth
- **chevron-right** — a simple greater-than arrow pointing right
- **arrow-left** — a simple less-than arrow pointing left
- **chevrons-right** — two greater-than arrows stacked, meaning "skip ahead"
- **zap** — a lightning bolt, sharp and angular
- **activity** — a zigzag line like a heart rate monitor
- **plus** — a simple plus sign
- **plus-circle** — a plus sign inside a circle
- **x-circle** — an X inside a circle, meaning "out"
- **circle** — an empty circle outline, meaning "ball" or "hit"
- **x** — a plain X, meaning "strike"
- **wind** — three curved horizontal lines, meaning "foul"
- **corner-up-left** — an arrow that curves up and left, meaning "undo"
- **skip-forward** — a right-pointing triangle with a vertical bar after it, meaning "end inning"
- **arrow-right-circle** — a circle with a right arrow inside, meaning "walk"
- **trending-up** — a line graph rising to the right
- **edit-2** — a pencil angled at 45 degrees
- **edit-3** — a slightly different pencil icon
- **users** — two stylized human silhouettes side by side
- **clock** — a circular clock face with hour and minute hands
- **database** — three stacked cylindrical disks, like a database symbol
- **home** — a simple house outline with a pitched roof
- **share** — three connected dots forming a branching network
- **more-vertical** — three vertical dots, the universal "menu" symbol
- **monitor** — a computer screen rectangle on a stand
- **sun** — a circle with eight short rays radiating outward
- **moon** — a crescent moon facing right
- **cloud** — a fluffy cloud outline
- **refresh-cw** — a circular arrow, same as rotate-cw
- **lock** — a padlock with a shackle on top
- **shield** — a heraldic shield shape
- **list** — four horizontal lines stacked
- **target** — concentric circles like a bullseye
- **wifi-off** — a WiFi symbol with a diagonal slash through it
- **check** — a bold checkmark
- **minus** — a short horizontal line
- **trending-up** — a rising line graph

All icons are rendered in the Primary Blue unless noted otherwise. Icons inside buttons are white. Icons inside the gold accent banner are the same dark navy as the text.

**What it looks like:**
A horizontal carousel of seven full-screen pages. You swipe between them, but the app actually disables free swiping — you advance by tapping buttons at the bottom. At the very bottom, a row of small dots shows your progress: seven dots, the active one is longer and blue, the rest are short gray lines.

The top third is empty space. Centered in the middle is the Batter Up logo: a small baseball bat crossing a baseball, with the words "Batter Up" next to it. Below the logo, in huge H1 text: "Welcome to Batter Up." Below that, in smaller body text: "A simple way to manage your lineup and keep the game moving."

Under the text is a white card with four rows. Each row has a small circular icon on the left — the first is a list icon, the second a target, the third a bar chart, the fourth a WiFi-off symbol — followed by a sentence in body text:
- "Build and save batting lineups"
- "Track current batter automatically"
- "Game stats and summaries"
- "Works fully offline — no account needed"

At the very bottom of the screen, spanning the full width, is a large blue button: "Get Started."

**Page 1 — Choose Your Style:**
Heading: "Choose Your Style." Subtext: "You can change this anytime in Settings."

Two large cards stacked vertically. The top card is for "Basic Mode." It has a lightning bolt icon in a light blue circle on the left. The card title is "Basic Mode" in H2. Below it, a caption says "Best for quick games." Below that, body text says "Track lineup, score, inning, current batter, and game summary." At the bottom left of this card is a small gold badge that says "Recommended for most coaches." The card has a blue border if selected.

The bottom card is "Advanced Mode." It has a zigzag activity icon instead of a lightning bolt. It says "Best for detailed tracking" and describes balls, strikes, pitch counts, hits, walks, outs, runs, player stats, and analytics. It has a thinner gray border.

At the bottom are two buttons side by side: "Back" on the left (outline style, smaller), "Next" on the right (solid blue, larger).

**Page 2 — Default Game Type:**
Heading: "Default Game Type." Subtext: "We'll use this to pre-fill your game rules."

**Page 3 — Choose Your Display:**
A large sun icon inside a light blue circle at the top center. Heading: "Choose Your Display."

Three rows, each with an icon on the left (a monitor, a sun, or a moon), a label in bold, a description in caption text, and a radio circle on the far right. The rows:
- System Default: "Follows your phone's display settings automatically"
- Light Mode: "High contrast. Best for outdoor use in bright sunlight."
- Dark Mode: "Easy on the eyes. Best for evening or night games."

**Page 4 — Auto-Backup:**
A shield icon in a light blue circle at the top. Heading: "Never lose your lineup." Subtext explains that enabling auto-backup will save lineups and games to iCloud (on iPhone) or Google Drive (on Android).

A white card with three rows explaining the backup:
- Cloud icon: "Backed up to [cloud name]"
- Refresh icon: "Happens automatically"
- Lock icon: "Private and free"

Two full-width buttons:
- "Enable Auto-Backup via [cloud name]" (solid blue)
- "Skip for now" (outline style)

**Page 5 — Help Is Always Here:**
A help-circle icon in a light blue circle. Heading: "Help is always here."

A white card with three rows:
- Zap icon: "Quick Start"
- Book-open icon: "Tutorial Library"
- Alert-circle icon: "Troubleshooting"

**Page 6 — You're All Set:**
A large blue circle in the center with a white checkmark inside. Heading: "You're all set!"

Below that, a summary sentence in muted text confirming the user's choices: "Basic Mode with Kid Pitch rules. Display set to System Default."

Three stacked buttons:
- "Create First Lineup" (solid blue, inside a card with a users icon above it)
- "Take a Quick Tour First" (outline style)

### Screen 2: Home

**What it looks like:**
A tall, vertically scrolling page. The background is the light gray (#F5F5F5). Everything is stacked from top to bottom with comfortable gaps between items.

**Top section:**
Centered near the top is the Batter Up logo — the same crossed bat and ball icon from onboarding, rendered at about two inches wide. Below the logo, in small caption text: "Build your lineup. Track the next batter. Keep the game moving."

**Mode badge:**
Just below the tagline, centered, is a small pill-shaped button. It has a thin blue border and a pale blue fill. Inside is a small icon — a lightning bolt if you're in Basic Mode, a zigzag if in Advanced Mode — followed by the text "Basic Mode" or "Advanced Mode" in blue. A tiny right-pointing chevron arrow sits at the right edge. Tapping it takes you to Settings.

**Game in Progress card (conditional):**
If there is an active unfinished game, a large card appears. The card has a dark navy background (#1A2C5B) and a thick gold (#F9A825) border. Inside:
- At the top left, a small glowing gold circle — like a status LED — followed by the text "GAME IN PROGRESS" in all-caps, gold, very bold, with letters spaced slightly apart.
- Below that, in large white text: "[Team Name] vs [Opponent Name]"
- Below that, two lines of smaller white text: "Top 2nd • 1 out" and "Team Name 4 • Opponent 3"
- If there is a current batter, another line: "Now batting: Mason Carter #12"
- At the bottom, spanning the card's width, a large gold button with dark navy text: "Resume Game" with a small play triangle icon on the left.

**Next scheduled game card (conditional):**
If there is an upcoming scheduled game, a horizontal card appears. It has a white background, a thin gray border, and rounded corners. On the far left is a light blue square (44 pixels) with a calendar icon in blue. To the right of the square:
- "NEXT GAME" in small blue bold text
- "vs Tigers" in body text
- "Tomorrow · 4:30 PM" in caption text
On the far right is a gray chevron pointing right. Tapping the whole card goes to the Schedule screen.

**The big button:**
A massive, full-width button. Solid primary blue (#0A74DA). Rounded corners (14 pixels). The text "Start Game" is centered in white, bold, 17 pixels. To the left of the text is a white play triangle icon at 20 pixels. The button has generous vertical padding — about 18 pixels on top and bottom — making it extremely easy to tap.

**Secondary grid:**
Below the big button, five square-ish cards arranged in a 2-column grid. Each card is white with a thin gray border, rounded corners, and sits about half the screen width with a small gap between columns. Inside each card:
- A light blue square icon container (48 pixels) with a primary-blue icon
- Below the icon, a label in 14-pixel semi-bold text

The five cards are:
- Top left: Bookmark icon. Label: "Saved Lineups"
- Top right: Calendar icon. Label: "Schedule"
- Middle left: Bar-chart-2 icon. Label: "Stats & History"
- Middle right: Help-circle icon. Label: "Help Center"
- Bottom left: Settings gear icon. Label: "Settings"

**Stats strip (conditional):**
If the user has any lineups or games, a thin horizontal bar appears. Centered in the bar:
- A number in large blue text (e.g., "3") with "Lineups" in caption below
- A thin vertical gray divider
- Another number (e.g., "7") with "Games" below

**Recent lineup chip (conditional):**
At the very bottom, a small horizontal row. On the left, a tiny clock icon in muted gray. Next to it, in small gray caption text: "Last used: Tigers Spring 2026." On the far right, in blue text: "View." The whole row is inside a thin-bordered white pill. It is clearly de-emphasized — the text is small and gray — so it does not compete with the big Start Game button.

### Screen 2a: Start Game Chooser (Modal)

**How it appears:**
The screen darkens behind it — a translucent black overlay covers everything. From the bottom of the screen, a white sheet slides up with rounded top corners (24 pixel radius). At the very top center of the sheet is a short gray horizontal bar (40 pixels wide, 4 pixels tall) — the visual cue that this is a bottom sheet you can dismiss by swiping down.

**Step 1 — Choose Mode:**
Centered at the top: "Start Game" in H2 bold text. Below it, in small gray caption: "How do you want to start?"

Three large horizontal cards stacked with 10-pixel gaps. Each card has:
- A light blue square icon container on the left (44 pixels, rounded 12-pixel corners)
- A bold title in body text
- A description in caption text below the title
- A gray chevron on the far right

Card 1 — New Game: Play-circle icon. Title: "New Game." Description: "Start a fresh game. Load an existing lineup or build a new one." Blue border.

Card 2 — Continue Game: Rotate-cw (refresh) icon. Title: "Continue Game." Description changes based on state: if there is an active game, it says "Pick up [Team] vs [Opponent] where you left off." If no game exists, it says "No game in progress." and the card is grayed out — the border becomes muted gray, the background becomes pale gray, opacity drops to 55 percent, and the chevron disappears.

Card 3 — Scheduled Game: Calendar icon. Title: "Scheduled Game." Description: "Next up: vs Tigers" or "Plan a game or start from an upcoming one." Blue border.

Below the cards, a full-width outline-style button: "Close."

**Step 2 — New Game → Choose Lineup:**
A back arrow appears in the top left. The title changes to "Choose Lineup." The caption says: "Use a saved lineup or build a new one."

Two cards:
- Bookmark icon: "Load Saved Lineup." Description: "Pick from 5 saved lineups" (or "No saved lineups yet — create one instead" if empty). If empty, this card is grayed out.
- Plus-circle icon: "Create New Lineup." Description: "Add players, set the batting order, and save." Always active.

A "Close" button at the bottom.

### Screen 3: Saved Lineups

**What it looks like:**
A list screen. White background. At the top, a header bar with a back arrow on the left, "Saved Lineups" (or "Choose Lineup" if in picker mode) in H2 centered, and a plus icon on the right for creating a new lineup.

If in picker mode, a thin banner appears below the header: light blue background, a play-circle icon, and the text "Tap a lineup to start a game" in blue.

**The list:**
Each lineup is a card. The card has a thin gray border, rounded 14-pixel corners, white background, and padding of about 16 pixels on all sides. Inside the card:
- Top row: The lineup name in H3 bold (e.g., "Tigers Spring 2026"). On the far right, if the lineup is favorited, a small bookmark icon in blue.
- Second row: Team name in caption text, or nothing if absent.
- Third row: Two small metadata chips side by side: "12 players" with a users icon, and "Used May 15" with a clock icon.
- Bottom row: A horizontal row of small circular action buttons:
  - Play-circle icon (start game) — blue background, white icon
  - Edit-2 icon (edit) — white background, gray border, dark icon
  - Duplicate icon (two overlapping squares) — same style as edit
  - Trash icon (delete) — white background, red border, red icon

**Empty state:**
If no lineups exist, the list area shows a large bookmark icon (48 pixels) in muted gray, centered. Below it: "No lineups saved yet" in H3. Below that: "Create a lineup to get started" in caption. Then a blue "Create Lineup" button.

### Screen 4: Lineup Editor

**What it looks like:**
A form screen with a white background. Header bar: back arrow, "New Lineup" or "Edit Lineup" in H2, and a "Save" text button in blue on the far right.

**Top card:**
A white card with two fields:
- "Lineup Name *" in label text. Below it, a text input box with a thin gray border, rounded corners, and placeholder text "e.g. Power Lineup, Balanced Order."
- "Team Name" label. Below it, another input with placeholder "Optional."

**Batting order section:**
A horizontal row with "Batting Order" in H3 on the left, and "12 active" in caption on the right.

**Player cards:**
Each player is a horizontal row. The row has a thin bottom border. On the far left, a small gray circle with the batting order number inside ("1", "2", "3"...) in bold. Next to it:
- Player name in body text, bold if active, grayed out if inactive
- Jersey number and position in caption text below the name (e.g., "#12 · SS")
- On the far right, if the player is active, a small green checkmark in a circle. If inactive, the checkmark is grayed out.

**Add player form:**
If the user taps "Add Player," a new white card expands below the list. It has:
- "New Player" in H3

- A row of small pill-shaped chips for quick position selection: P, C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, LF, CF, RF, DH, OF, IF. Each chip is a small rounded rectangle. If tapped, it fills with blue and the text turns white.
- Two buttons at the bottom: "Cancel" (outline) and "Add Player" (solid blue)

At the very bottom, a full-width blue "Save Lineup" button.

### Screen 6: Live Game

**This is the heart of the app. It is the most complex screen.**

**Top header — The Scorecard:**
A tall block, about 25% of the screen, with a dark navy background (#1A2C5B). This is the visual anchor.

Top row of the scorecard:
- On the far left, a vertical stack labeled "INNING" in tiny gray text, then a huge white number (e.g., "Top 2" — the triangle symbol is either an upward-pointing hollow triangle or a downward one), then "of 6" in tiny text.
- In the center, two large numbers separated by a long dash. The left number is the user's score in bold gold (#F9A825), about 40 pixels tall. The right number is the opponent's score in white, same size. Above each number is the team name in tiny gray all-caps text, truncated to 10 letters. Below each number are tiny minus and plus buttons for manual score adjustment — the minus is a short horizontal line, the plus is a plus sign, both in semi-transparent white.
- On the far right, two small circular icon buttons stacked vertically: a three-dot menu (more-vertical) and a settings gear.

**Count strip:**
Below the score, a horizontal row of small panels. In Basic Mode, only two panels appear:
- "OUTS" label with a row of small dots below it. Filled dots are gold; empty dots are semi-transparent. For 3 outs, you might see 2 filled and 1 empty.
- "RUNS THIS INNING" label with a gold number.

In Advanced Mode, four panels appear:
- "BALLS" with green dots
- "STRIKES" with red dots
- "OUTS" with gold dots
- "RUNS" with "2/6" if a run limit is set, or just the number

**Demo mode banner (conditional):**
If demo mode is on, a thin yellow strip appears below the scorecard with an activity icon and orange text: "SANDBOX MODE — Stats not saved."

**Current batter banner:**
A full-width horizontal bar with a bright gold background (#F9A825). About 15% of the screen height.
- On the left: "NOW BATTING" in tiny bold dark text. Below it, the batter's name in very large bold dark text (e.g., "Mason Carter"). Below that, jersey number and position in smaller text (e.g., "#12 · Shortstop").
- On the right side of the banner, a small dark box with "On Deck" in tiny text and the next batter's name below it.

**Lineup list:**
A scrollable list of all active players. Each row is a full-width horizontal bar.
- The current batter's row is solid primary blue (#0A74DA) with a thick gold vertical stripe on the left edge. The number badge on the left is semi-transparent white. The player's name is white and bold. An "UP" label in gold appears on the far right.
- The on-deck player's row is light blue (#E8F1FB). The name is dark. "On Deck" appears on the far right in gray.
- All other rows are white with a thin bottom border. The name is regular weight dark text.

The list auto-scrolls so the current batter is centered when the batter changes.

**Action buttons:**
A large area at the bottom, about 30% of the screen, with a white card background.

In Basic Mode, two rows of buttons:
- Top row (four large buttons): Hit (green circle icon), Out (red X-circle icon), Walk (blue arrow-right-circle icon), Run (gold plus-circle icon). Each button is a vertical stack: icon on top, label below. Buttons are large squares with rounded corners.
- Bottom row (three smaller buttons): Next (blue chevrons-right icon), End Inning (gray skip-forward icon), Undo (gray corner-up-left icon).

In Advanced Mode, four rows:
- Row 1: Ball (green circle), Strike (red X), Foul (orange wind icon), Out (dark red X-circle)
- Row 2: Single, Double, Triple, Home Run — four small text-only pills
- Row 3: Strikeout, Walk, HBP, Run — four small text-only pills
- Row 4: Next, End Half-Inning, Undo, RBI — four small buttons

Every button press gives a short haptic vibration.

**Game Menu (modal):**
When the three-dot menu is tapped, a modal slides up from the bottom with a dark overlay behind it. The modal is a white sheet with rounded top corners. Inside:
- "Game Menu" in H2 at the top
- Four rows, each with an icon on the left and text on the right:
  - Edit-2 icon: "Edit Lineup"
  - Settings icon: "Game Options"
  - Activity icon: "Switch to Advanced Mode" (or "Switch to Basic Mode")
  - X-circle icon: "End Game" (in red text)
- A "Cancel" ghost button at the bottom

**Confirm modals:**
When ending the game or switching modes, a smaller centered modal appears. It has a white card with rounded corners. Title in H2, body text in body size, and two buttons side by side: "Cancel" (outline) and "Confirm" (solid, either blue or red depending on danger level).

### Screen 7: Game Summary

**What it looks like:**
A tall scrolling screen with a white background. Header bar: a home icon on the left, "Game Summary" in H2 centered, a share icon on the right.

A large navy-blue card with rounded corners, taking up most of the top half of the screen. Centered at the top is a pill-shaped badge: "WIN" in white text on a green background, or "LOSS" on red, or "TIE" on gold. Below the badge, the game date in tiny gray text.

In the center, two enormous numbers side by side. Your team's score is in gold, about 52 pixels tall, ultra-bold. The opponent's score is in white, same size. A long dash separates them. Above each score, the team name in tiny gray text.

**Team Stats card:**
A white card with "Team Stats" in H3. Below it, a 2x2 grid of small rounded boxes:
- Hits (number in blue)
- Walks (number in blue)
- Strikeouts (number in blue)

## Part 6: Interactions and Animations

### Transitions
When navigating between screens, the new screen slides in from the right. The old screen slides out to the left. This is the standard iOS/Android navigation feel.

### Bottom sheets
Modals that slide up from the bottom have a spring animation — they overshoot slightly and settle. The background darkens with a fade.

### Button presses
Every button visually shrinks to 95% of its size for 100 milliseconds when tapped, then bounces back. This gives tactile feedback even before the haptic vibration.

### Count dots
When a ball or strike is recorded, the dot appears with a small scale-up animation — it grows from nothing to full size in 200 milliseconds.

### Batter scroll
When the batter changes, the lineup list smoothly scrolls so the current batter row lands about one-third down from the top of the visible area. The scroll takes about 300 milliseconds.

### Undo
When undo is tapped, the last event reverses its effect with no special animation — the score or count simply decrements instantly. The haptic gives the feedback.

## Part 8: Accessibility Description

For a blind user with VoiceOver or TalkBack:

- Every button is labeled with its action. The big "Start Game" button reads: "Start Game, button."
- The scorecard header reads the full score and inning in one breath: "Inning top 2 of 6. Your team 4, opponent 3."
- The current batter banner is announced automatically when the batter changes: "Now batting, Mason Carter, number 12, shortstop."
- The count dots are described as: "Balls, 2 of 4. Strikes, 1 of 3. Outs, 2 of 3."
- The lineup list reads each player's name, number, position, and whether they are batting now or on deck.
- The action buttons all have clear labels: "Record ball," "Record strike," "Record out," "Next batter," "Undo last event."
- The modal sheets announce themselves as "Bottom sheet, Start Game chooser."
- The walkthrough modal announces the step number and content.
